Providing users with helpful tips and daily news Tips and News 1. Soak Soak the tree while you work to ensure the root ball is soaked through, as a dry root ball could reject water from the surrounding soil. 2. Dig It’s important to dig a hole that’s at least twice the size of the existing root ball of the tree. Depth is more important than width in most cases. 3. Improve Improve your soil to help the plant survive in the long-term. In clay soils, add some gypsum to improve the soil structure and drainage, and well-rotted compost to provide organic matter and further structure. 4. Decant Take the plant out of its pot in a way that keeps the roots as intact as possible. Root disturbance in healthy stock will shock the plant. 5. Plant Place the plant in the hole, and back-fill it with soil to cover the roots. Make sure the soil around the plant is firm. 6. Water Water the plant in. This ensures that the soil settles around the plant, and the roots get good contact with the soil.
